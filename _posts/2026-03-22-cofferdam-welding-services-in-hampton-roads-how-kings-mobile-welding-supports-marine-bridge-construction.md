A **cofferdam** is a temporary watertight structure, typically built from interlocking sheet piles, that allows construction crews to pump out water and work in dry conditions.

While the sheet piles themselves are driven into place and interlocked, **welding plays a major role in everything that happens inside and around the cofferdam.**

***

## Where Welding Is Required in Cofferdam Construction

### 🔧 1. Internal Bracing Systems (Walers & Struts)

One of the most critical structural components of a cofferdam is its internal bracing system.

- Horizontal **walers** reinforce the sheet pile walls
- **Struts and cross-bracing** prevent collapse under pressure
- Field-fit connections often require **on-site welding**

Kings Mobile Welding specializes in:

- Beam-to-beam welding
- Structural plate connections
- Reinforcement of existing bracing systems

### ⚙️ 3. Penetrations & Pipe Pass-Through Welding

Cofferdams often require:

- Pipe penetrations
- Tie-back systems
- Utility pass-throughs

These require:

- Reinforcement plates
- Seal welds around penetrations
- Structural tie-ins to maintain integrity

***

### 🛠️ 4. Sheet Pile Repairs & Attachments

Although sheet piles interlock, they still require welding for:

- Damage repair
- Adding lifting lugs or connection points
- Installing brackets or tie-ins

***

### 🌊 5. Working in High-Pressure, High-Risk Environments

Cofferdam environments are:

- Tight, confined spaces
- High-pressure due to surrounding water loads
- Often on critical path schedules

That’s why contractors rely on:
