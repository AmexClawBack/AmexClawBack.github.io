## What Is an Elastomeric Expansion Dam?

An **elastomeric expansion dam** is a flexible bridge joint system designed to allow movement between bridge sections while maintaining a watertight seal.

These systems accommodate:

- Thermal expansion in heat
- Contraction in cold temperatures
- Movement from live traffic loads

Most are designed for **3”–4” of movement**, making them a standard solution on DOT and infrastructure projects throughout Virginia.

***

## How Bridge Expansion Joint Systems Are Built

Bridge expansion joint assemblies typically consist of:

- **Steel edge rails / armor angles**
- **Elastomeric (rubber) seal**
- **Embedded steel or anchorage into the deck**

The **steel components are where AWS D1.5 certified welding is required**, and proper installation is critical to the long-term performance of the joint.

## Why Expansion Joint Welding Is Critical

Bridge joints are one of the highest-wear areas on any structure.

They are constantly exposed to:

- Heavy traffic impact
- Weather and water infiltration
- Continuous expansion and contraction

If welding is not done correctly:

- Water intrusion can damage the deck
- Structural components deteriorate faster
- The joint fails prematurely

This is why most bridge joint work requires:
👉 **AWS D1.5 certified bridge welding**
